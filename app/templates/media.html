Happy Voting is based on the need for more voter participation in the upcoming European Elections on May 20-25. There are two different ways to approach this issue: one is through increased education and informative projects that explain the European Elections and make sure that young people are involved in the decision making process.

The second approach is that young people are informed about the basic minimum when it comes to the European Parliamentary Elections. That means simply knowing that the elections exist, as well as the month in which they are held. It also means raising awareness and rendering them more attractive to young people and tailoring the message to their entertainment expectations.

That’s why Happy Voting plays an important role online. Instead of educating and informing about the political choices, we start from the bottom and try to portray the elections differently. The reason for choosing the entertainment industry as a point of reference for this campaign was precisely to ensure that – at the very least – young people are attracted to the idea of voting. The next step is of course providing further and better educational tools to understand the choices available to young people.

Lets make voting more inclusive and fun!
